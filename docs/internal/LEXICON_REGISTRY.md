# Levershare Lexicon Registry

The Levershare Lexicon Registry is the internal record of concepts that define, support, or may eventually become part of the Levershare knowledge system.

The Registry intentionally contains more concepts than the published Lexicon.

A concept enters the Registry when it is considered important enough to preserve, examine, or develop further.

Only concepts that become sufficiently mature, stable, and aligned with the broader Levershare philosophy are promoted to official Lexicon entries.

This document is internal and is not intended for public distribution.

---

## Registry Principles

- The Registry is broader than the published Lexicon.
- Registration does not guarantee future publication.
- Concepts should not be promoted solely because they are technically implemented.
- Conceptual maturity is more important than development order.
- Published definitions should remain understandable outside their immediate technical implementation.
- New concepts may be added whenever they are considered important enough to preserve.
- Concepts may be revised, merged, renamed, postponed, or archived as the Levershare ecosystem evolves.

---

## Status Definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Published` | The concept has an official Lexicon entry. |
| `Planned` | The concept is expected to become a Lexicon entry but has not yet been fully developed. |
| `Research` | The concept requires further conceptual discussion, research, or clarification. |
| `Archived` | The concept is no longer actively planned but is retained for historical or reference purposes. |

---

## Priority Definitions

| Priority | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `High` | Important for explaining the current Levershare system or its foundational architecture. |
| `Medium` | Valuable for expanding understanding but not immediately required. |
| `Low` | Potentially useful in the future but not currently essential. |

# Promotion Criteria

A registered concept may become an official Lexicon entry when:

- its definition is sufficiently stable;
- its role within Levershare is clearly understood;
- it can be explained independently from temporary implementation details;
- it is consistent with the current Whitepaper and published Essays;
- it does not duplicate an existing Lexicon concept;
- its terminology is expected to remain useful as the ecosystem evolves;
- its related concepts and conceptual dependencies are sufficiently clear.

---

# Maintenance Rules

When a new concept emerges:

1. Add it to the most appropriate layer.
2. Assign a provisional status and priority.
3. Record known dependencies.
4. Add a short note explaining why the concept should be preserved.
5. Do not write a full Lexicon definition inside the Registry.
6. Promote it only after sufficient research and conceptual discussion.
7. Update its status to `Published` after the Lexicon page is live and verified.

When an existing concept changes:

1. Update the Registry note if its role has materially changed.
2. Review its dependencies.
3. Review related Lexicon metadata.
4. Increment the Lexicon entry revision when published metadata or content changes.
5. Preserve obsolete concepts as `Archived` when historical context remains useful.
